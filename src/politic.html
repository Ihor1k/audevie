Privacy Policy

Effective as of August 21, 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how AUDEVIE TRADING CORP. (hereinafter referred to as “Audevie”, “we”, “our”, or “us”), a company incorporated under the laws of the British Virgin Islands, collects, uses, and protects personal data of individuals who interact with us through our website or services.

We are committed to safeguarding your privacy and handling your data in a transparent and secure manner, in accordance with applicable data protection principles under the laws of the British Virgin Islands.

2. What We Collect

We only collect personal data necessary to provide our services or respond to inquiries. This may include:

• Full name
• Email address
• Company / organization (if provided)
• Phone number (if provided)
• Message details or other information voluntarily submitted through our contact form

3. Why We Collect It

Your personal data is collected and processed in order to:

• Respond to inquiries and provide requested information
• Communicate with you regarding our services
• Maintain proper records of interactions with clients and potential clients
• Comply with legal or regulatory obligations applicable under BVI law

4. Who Receives Your Personal Data

Your personal data may be processed by authorized employees of Audevie and, where necessary, trusted partners or service providers acting on our behalf (e.g., hosting providers, communication tools).

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties. We may disclose data if legally required by a competent authority under applicable BVI law.

5. Transfer of Personal Data

As a company based in the British Virgin Islands, your personal data may be transferred to and stored in jurisdictions outside your country of residence. Where such transfers occur, we will take reasonable steps to ensure your data remains adequately protected in line with this Policy.

6. Data Retention

We will retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Policy, or as required by applicable law. Once data is no longer needed, it will be securely deleted or anonymized.

7. Your Rights

You have the following rights in relation to your personal data, subject to applicable BVI law:

• Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. • Correction: Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete data. • Erasure: Request the deletion of your data where there is no legal basis for us to continue processing it. • Objection: Object to processing of your data where we rely on legitimate interest. • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for processing at any time (this will not affect processing carried out before withdrawal).

To exercise these rights, please contact us at info@audevie.pro.

8. Complaints

AIf you believe that we have not handled your personal data appropriately, you should first contact us directly at info@audevie.pro.

If you remain unsatisfied, you may seek recourse under the applicable laws of the British Virgin Islands, including through the courts or other competent regulatory authorities established in the territory.

9. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in law, our practices, or other business needs. Any updates will be posted on our website with the revised effective date. We encourage you to review this Policy periodically to remain informed about how we protect your personal data.
